Synopsis

The two datasets I have chosen displays CO2 emmissions in metric tons from major companies across the country. They allow us to see the outputs of companies that produce many different types of substances including chemicals, metals, minerals, electricity, food and more. The interest I have in choosing two very similiar charts is the ability to compare them over time. For a large scale project it would be beneficial to collect the same tables of data over a ten or twenty year span in order to see how effectively we have been dealing with the emmissions from our factories. I believe visualizing this data could display the factory types that produce the most emmissions which would be useful in finding the factories that need the most attention in order to reduce emmissions. You could even go further and graph the data on a picture of the United States using the longitudes and latitudes input in order to place the data based on its real life location in order to display the emmissions based on the state that factory is in. This could give further insight into what areas are taking the making the most changes to their emmission sizes and what areas aren't.

Other Oppurtunities

With a little more research and a few more datasets based around the measures taken to reduce emissions at these factories we could also discover which brands of emission reducing filters work the best for certain factory types. We could also compare the emissions datasets to the growth in global warming over time in order to identify if the changes we are making are helping to slow or decrease the changes possibly using a heat map visualization.
